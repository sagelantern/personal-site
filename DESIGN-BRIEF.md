# Design Consultation Brief: yash-kothari.com

## Who This Is For

Yash Kothari. 32. Indian-American. Lives in NYC (Upper West Side) with his wife Shivantika and son Sohum (2 years old), second child due August 2026.

## The Full Picture (Not Just the Resume)

**Professional surface**: CEO of Kindred (AI performance management), Senior VP at Pioneer Fund (VC), Wharton undergrad, YC alum, Forbes contributor, work in WSJ and NYT. Two-time founder.

**But that's not who he actually is right now.** He's in a deliberate transition away from achiever identity. The professional stuff is real but it's the shell, not the core. Here's the deeper layer:

- **Deepening Buddhist meditation practice** is the central thread of his life right now. Daily 1-hour morning sits, afternoon meditation, studying with teachers. Vipassana is the foundation. He's exploring jhana practice. This isn't a wellness hobby — it's the container for everything else.
- **Family is first.** Father to a toddler, another on the way. Family dinner at 7pm is sacred. Presence with his son matters more than any business outcome.
- **Indian heritage runs deep.** Born in India, carries the philosophy of *atithi devo bhava* (the guest is sacred) into his hospitality writing. His wife wrote "Bombay || New York" — a book about the duality of being rooted in both places. The family straddles Mumbai and NYC.
- **He thinks in frameworks and tradeoffs** but is trying to feel more and analyze less. His therapist and meditation practice are both working on this.
- **Writer.** Not a content marketer. Actual essays about meaning, imperfection, presence. Forbes, WSJ, NYT. Also a hospitality journal (Stay Nivas) that reviews hotels through feeling rather than amenities.
- **Creative side.** Makes comics blending Indian and NYC imagery. Appreciates craft and design. Prestige TV dramas. Epic fantasy novels.
- **Introvert.** Social posting feels hard. The site should speak for itself without requiring constant updates.
- **Eulogy values over resume values.** His north star is David Brooks' "Moral Bucket List" — to radiate inner light. Selfless, loving, present. Deeply good.

## What the Site Should Feel Like

This is a personal site, not a startup landing page. It should feel like walking into someone's home — warm, considered, personal. Not performative.

**Tensions to hold:**
- Professional credibility AND spiritual depth (without being preachy or woo)
- Indian heritage AND New York modernity
- Simplicity AND richness
- Confidence AND humility
- A builder's precision AND a meditator's stillness

**Reference points for vibe:**
- Boutique hotel lobbies (Alila, Andaz, Beekman) — not flashy luxury, but considered warmth
- The quiet confidence of someone who has nothing left to prove
- Derek Sivers' site (minimal, personal, opinionated)
- Craig Mod's site (beautiful typography, writer-first, deeply personal)
- Indian textile patterns and warm tones (not Bollywood bright, more like hand-block prints on linen)

## Current Design Assessment

The current site uses:
- Fonts: DM Sans (sans), Crimson Text (serif), Darker Grotesque (labels)
- Colors: ivory (#F7F4F0), midnight (#002835), teal (#007B76), sage (#C8D4C9)
- Layout: Single column, 680px max-width, clean and minimal
- Built with Astro + Tailwind

It's clean but generic. It could be anyone's personal site. Nothing about it says "this person meditates every morning, grew up in India, thinks about presence, loves his family deeply." The design needs to carry more of who he is without becoming cluttered or precious.

## The Missing Piece: The 7 Energy

The first design consultation landed the contemplative side well but flattened Yash into it. He's NOT a monk who happens to code. He's an **Enneagram 7 and Aries who chose stillness** — and that's way more interesting.

The real brand tension isn't "achiever vs practitioner." It's:

**Stillness AND restlessness living in the same person.**

Yash is creative, full of ideas, bold, spontaneous, playful. He makes comics. He loves epic fantasy. He gets excited about new projects. He's an Aries with a type-7's hunger for experience. AND he sits for an hour every morning in Vipassana. AND he's building discipline and presence.

The site should celebrate this tension as the feature, not resolve it. Both should be visibly true at once.

This means:
- The overall system is warm, grounded, editorial — but with **pockets of play, spark, and creative energy** that break through
- Like a meditation room with a comic pinned to the wall
- Like a hand-block printed textile with one unexpected, alive color
- Moments of irreverence, wit, boldness alongside the depth
- The homepage should NOT read like a dharma teacher's bio. It should feel like someone who contains multitudes and is honest about all of them
- Typography needs more contrast — warmth AND bite
- The About page especially should celebrate the contradiction: YC founder who sits for an hour every morning, Aries who chose Vipassana, type 7 building discipline
- The palette needs a "voltage" color — something more alive than terracotta, a spark that says "this person has energy, not just wisdom"

## What I Want From This Consultation

1. **Brand identity**: Colors, typography, visual language that reflects the FULL Yash — the contemplative depth AND the creative restlessness. The tension is the brand.
2. **Design direction**: How do you hold stillness and spark in the same design system? Where do the pockets of play live?
3. **Specific recommendations**: Font pairings (warmth + bite), color palette (grounded warmth + one voltage accent), texture, pattern, micro-interactions
4. **Content architecture**: Is the current page structure right? What's missing? How does the About page tell the contradictions story?
5. **Implementation plan**: What to build, in what order

## Technical Constraints

- No heavy JS frameworks needed
- Images are fine but not required for the homepage
